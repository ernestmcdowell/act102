Culture

Culture Overview

Da Nang Vietnam is the fifth largest city in Vietnam by population. It is also the the cultural and educational hub for all of central Vietnam. Tourism is a major component in Da Nang's economy, attracting millions of people every year. Sites include, The Dragon Bridge, the Bà Nà Mountains, the Hands of God, and the many active Pagoda temples and ancient ruins.

History

Ancient Da Nang

The history of Da Nang dates back to 192 AD to the Champa kingdom. Da Nang has been heavily contested throughout history. land disputes between ancient civilizations in the area date back over several centuries.

Vietnam War

During the Vietnam War, Da Nang international airport was used as a base of operations for the South Vietnamese and US Air Force. It was also the most active airport in the world during the war. On an average day during the war the airport would handle over 2500 air operations.

Economy

Vietnam Currency

The currency in Vietnam is the Vietnam dong. $1.00 USD is equivalent to around $22,500 VND. Da Nang's primary economy drivers are Agriculture, forestry, trade, and tourism. From tourism alone Da Nang generated over $850 million USD (19.4 trillion VND) in 2017.

Economy Drivers

  • Agricultural Exports
    • 45,000 tons of rice
    • 41,000 tons of fish
  • Industry
    • Da Nang exports a wide array of industrial products such as:
      • Machinery
      • Fabrics and clothes
      • Medical Tablets
  • Trade
    • Da Nang's main marketplace is the Hàn Market

Anthem

Anthem History

The Vietnam national anthem Tiến Quân Ca was written by a composer named Van Cao. In english Tiến Quân Ca translates to Army March. The anthem was officially adopted by the Democratic Republic of Vietnam in 1945. Even though the anthem is written with 2 verses, only the first verse is included in the official national anthem.

Army March

        Armies of Vietnam, forward!
        With one single determination to save our Fatherland,
        Our hurried steps resound on the long and arduous road.
        Our flag, red with the blood of victory, bears the spirit of the country.
        The distant rumbling of the guns mingles with our marching song.
        The path to glory is built by the bodies of our foes.
        Overcoming all hardships, together we build our resistance bases.
        Ceaselessly for the People’s cause let us struggle,
        Let us hasten to the battlefield!
        Onward! All together advancing!
        For one eternal Vietnam.
        (The sixth line was original: “We swear to flay the enemies and drink their blood” This was changed to current wordings in 1955 by the Fifth Plenum of the First National Assembly.)
